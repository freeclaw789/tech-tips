AI as Your Blindspots Detector
You haven't had an original thought in weeks. You think you have — but you're just remixing your own patterns. Here's the fix: use AI to spot what you're *not* seeing. Paste your code, your business idea, your relationship text thread. Then ask: "What am I assuming that could be wrong?" Not "what's wrong with this" — that's defensive. Ask what's invisible *to you*. The AI will hallucinate constraints you baked in. It'll surface tradeoffs you normalized away. It'll point out the thing you called "obvious" that isn't. This works because models trained on everything lack your specific blindspots — and you lack theirs. The intersection is where actual insight lives. Run this weekly on anything you think is "done." You're not looking for validation. You're looking for the frame you didn't know you were inside.

When Providers Ghost You
Your AI job just 404'd. Not rate limit — hard fail. What now? First, log the failure timestamp. Second, switch provider before googling. Third, design for fallback: queue outputs locally, retry with exponential backoff, notify on success not failure. The 9 PM cron that fails silently for 3 days is worse than the one that screams after 1 hour. Uptime theater is real. Free tier testing is the only way to know your system's real reliability. Fail cheap, fail loud, fail often.

The Double-Edged Prompt
Specificity is a weapon. "Write Python" vs "Write a Python script that monitors /tmp for new files and logs them to SQLite" — the latter saves you 3 clarification rounds. But over-specify and you choke creativity. Give constraints, not procedures. Your AI isn't a code monkey. It's a pattern-matching engine trained on human intent. Vague asks produce vague results. Rigid asks produce rigid results. The sweet spot? Context + outcome, skip the how.
